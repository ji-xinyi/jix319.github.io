What Is A Union?

A union is an organized group of workers who act collectively to build power and improve their working conditions. Through a union, workers can bargain collectively for improvements to wages, benefits, and other terms of employment, ultimately securing those improvements in a union contract.

Why are College Students Unionising?

College campuses have increasingly outsourced their labour to students themselves, as they staff essential parts of college and play a part in creating the 'college experience', such as library staff, teaching courses, building community, amongst others. With ever-increasing tuition costs, students are looking to demand better and fairer terms of employment. Historically, colleges have also been resistant to student employees unionising.
According to this 2018 report,70% of college students work. ‘But a true portrait of today’s “working learners” depicts a far more varied population that increasingly includes students who work full time while going to school, as well as those who work part-time, but are logging more than 15 hours each week’

“About 14 million students are working learners, defined as individuals both active in the labor market and formally enrolled at postsecondary institutions. “

What do Student Workers Want?

  • Better Working Conditions, such as more control over shift timings and responsive management
  • Fair and Just Compensation. Student positions, such as resident assistants, require enormous amounts of effort and long shifts. Paying students a fair wage would reduce the need to get a second job to make ends meet and reduce burnout.
  • Job Security. Some positions require an annual application process, despite student employees performing well in the position.
  • Benefits. Like any other job, benefits such as Seniority Pay, Health Insurance, and more are amongst some of the things that can incentivise better job performance and employee satisfaction.
